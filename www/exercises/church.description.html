In the lambda calculus, natural numbers (0, 1, 2, and so on) are typically represented as Church numerals. In this encoding:

  • 0 = \s.\z.z
  • 1 = \s.\z.s(z)
  • 2 = \s.\z.s(s(z))
  • 3 = \s.\z.s(s(s(z)))

and so on.

Try writing a few larger natural numbers and evaluate them, interpreting the result as a Church numeral.
